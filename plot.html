Impact on plot

At first glance, Ophelia's role in Hamlet's plot is unclear. However, many people say her role is to be an easy way for Hamlet and Polonius to get what they want.

examples

Act 1

It fits your wisdom so far to believe it as he in his particular act and place may give his saying deed, which is no further than the main voice of Denmark goes withal. Then weigh what loss your honor may sustain if with too credent ear you list his songs, or lose your heart, or your chaste treasure open to his unmastered importunity. Fear it, Ophelia. Fear it, my dear sister, and keep you in the rear of your affection, out of the shot and danger of desire.

Shakespeare (25-36)

This quote demonstrates other characters' suspitions of Hamlet. Laertes warns Ophelia about him, however, we don't directly see it from Hamlet yet.

Act 2

• My lord, as I was sewing in my closet, Lord Hamlet, with his doublet all unbraced; No hat upon his head; his stockings fouled, Ungartered, and down-gyvèd to his ankle; Pale as his shirt; his knees knocking each other; And with a look so piteous in purport as if he had been loosèd out of hell to speak of horrors— he comes before me.

Shakespeare (2.1.79-84)

This quote is proof that Hamlet is using Ophelia. Rather than speaking to her, he stares at her, with clothes either dirty or half off. That gives the idea that he is using her for sex.

Act 3

Sweet Gertrude, leave us too, for we have closely sent for Hamlet hither, that he, as ’twere by accident, may here affront Ophelia. Her father and myself (lawful espials) Will so bestow ourselves that, seeing unseen, we may of their encounter frankly judge, and gather by him, as he is behaved, If ’t be the affliction of his love or That thus he suffers for.

Shakespeare ((3.1.29))

This quote demonstrates how Polonius is using Ophelia for Claudius's needs. Claudius is technically Polonius's boss, therefore, Ophelia is helping Polonius fullfill duties in his occupation.

The other thing Ophelia is used for is demonstrating the unfairness in Elizabethan society. If people were remotely different from the normal, Christian values, you were considered less than.

Example

Give me leave. Here lies the water. Good. Here stands the man. Good. If the man go to this water and drown himself, it is, will he nill he, he goes. Mark you that. But if the water come to him and drown him, he drowns not himself. Argal, he that is not guilty of his own death shortens not his own life.

Shakespeare (5.1.20)

The reason this quote demonstrates this is because of the fact they are talking about Ophelia's death as if it is less of a tragity because her death was a suicide.
